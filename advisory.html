Selective Engagements

Technical Advisory

I take on a small number of advisory engagements alongside my primary focus on full-time Director and VP of Engineering roles. The work is hands-on and specific: AI-native platform strategy, architecture and modernization reviews, M&A technical due diligence and integration, and engineering organization assessments. If you're working through one of those problems at the executive level, I'm worth a conversation.

Who I Work With

I work best with leadership teams at seed-through-growth-stage SaaS companies facing a specific inflection point: a planned AI platform investment, a recent acquisition to integrate, an engineering organization scaling past the point where informal processes still work, or a cloud modernization with real cost and performance stakes.

This is not general-purpose consulting work. I'm not a good fit for staff augmentation, role backfill, or broad "help us improve engineering" mandates without a defined problem at the center. The engagements that go well are ones where the leadership team has a clear question and wants senior engineering judgment applied to it.

Engagement Types

AI Platform & Adoption Strategy

I've built a multi-tenant AI platform on AWS Bedrock from the ground up — RAG pipelines, agent orchestration, prompt management, and LLM observability — for a production SaaS environment at scale. I also built the organizational side: a three-tier AI adoption framework (Baseline, Accelerator, Champion) that drove a measured 20% increase in developer velocity across the engineering team. If you're making the architectural and organizational decisions about how your platform and your team engage with AI, I can help you make them with more clarity and less rework.

Typical cadence
4-8 week engagement, or ongoing monthly advisory if the work warrants it.
Primary outcome
A documented AI platform architecture and adoption strategy aligned to your team's current state — including the governance and tooling decisions you'll need to make before you scale.

Architecture & Modernization Review

I led the migration of a production SaaS platform from a monolithic .NET architecture to containerized Python microservices and AWS serverless infrastructure — ECS, Lambda, Step Functions — resulting in a 350% increase in system throughput and a 25% reduction in infrastructure cost. That experience informs how I assess modernization decisions: not just whether the architecture is technically sound, but whether the migration path is realistic given the team's capacity and the business's tolerance for risk. I focus on the trade-offs that tend to be underweighted in these decisions — migration sequencing, operational complexity, and cost at scale.

Typical cadence
2-4 week discrete engagement with a written deliverable report.
Primary outcome
A prioritized modernization roadmap ranking your architecture options by ROI, risk, and migration complexity — written for both the engineering team and the leadership team reading it.

M&A Technical Due Diligence & Integration

I've navigated M&A from both sides of the table: as CTO of an acquired B2B SaaS company, and then as Director of Engineering at the acquirer leading the full technical integration. That integration completed in 12 weeks with zero customer churn and 92% employee retention — in an environment where both outcomes were genuinely at risk. I understand what technical due diligence actually needs to surface, what post-acquisition integration planning consistently underestimates, and how to keep an engineering team functional through the ambiguity that M&A creates.

Typical cadence
Due diligence is typically 1-2 weeks; post-acquisition integration planning and oversight spans weeks to months depending on scope.
Primary outcome
For due diligence: a technical risk assessment covering architecture, team, debt, and operational readiness. For integration: a sequenced integration roadmap with dependency mapping and retention risk flags.

Engineering Organization Assessment

At SugarCRM, I scaled the engineering organization by 150% — from a team that had outgrown its informal structures to a 26-person global organization with five distinct teams, a functioning management layer, and 90% retention through that entire growth period. That required deliberate work on team structure, career laddering, promotion pipelines, engineering process maturity, and how the organization communicated across time zones. I bring that same framework to assessments: I'm looking at whether your org is structured for the scale it's heading toward, not just the scale it's at.

Typical cadence
2-3 week assessment with a recommendations deliverable.
Primary outcome
A written assessment of your engineering organization's current state against the scale you're targeting — covering team structure, process maturity, retention risk, and the specific changes that will have the most leverage.

Ongoing Advisory

Some of the most useful advisory work isn't a discrete project — it's having a senior engineering leader available to think through decisions before they get made. I work with a small number of CEOs, CTOs, and engineering leaders on a standing basis: quarterly planning, architecture decisions, team and hiring strategy, difficult leadership situations. This is judgment work, not implementation work — the value is in the perspective and the conversation, not in deliverables.

Typical cadence
Monthly or quarterly retainer, with sessions scheduled as needed.
Primary outcome
A consistent senior engineering sounding board for the decisions your leadership team is working through — available before the decision is made, not after.
